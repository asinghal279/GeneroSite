ROADIES
(Hunt for a G.O.A.T)

  • Form filling

    • A form is made up where information of the participants is taken and certain question are asked on rational thinking of the students.

  • Group Discussion

    • A group of 8-10 participants is made where they compete on speaking ,ideas and confidence. The deserving participants are shortlisted from each group for the next round.

  • Personal Interview

    • Participants are questioned face to face by the judges about their personality and the form they have filled up.

  • Tasks

    • Here the number of tasks take place , followed by vote out. Tasks will examine physical stamina, strength, quick and clever plan of action.

TUG OF WAR
(War of Power)

  • Each team in Tug of War competition will consist of 8 to 10 people.
  • Both teams pull the rope, the winner being the team who manage to pull the mark on the rope closest to their opponents over the centre line.

NEWSPAPER DANCE
(Together & strong)

Each pair stands on the newspaper. They may only touch the newspaper but not the floor. As soon as the music is turned on, the pairs have to "dance" as best as possible. Any team touching the floor is out of the game. After some time, the music stops and the referee orders the newspaper to be folded in half (teams may step on the floor during that time). Then, the music (and dancing) starts again. The team that manages to "survive" the dancing as last pair has of course won the game.

BULL’S EYE

Participants need to match up the given magic number in three chances. Each participant will create her/his own series of targets and point system by setting up circles outside of each other, starting with the middle circle (bull’s eye) (e.g., the bull’s eye is 0.5 m in diameter, the next circle outside the middle circle is 1 m in diameter, the last circle is 1.5 m in diameter on the ground and scores are given in random arrangements. If the participant’s scores matches with any of the three magic number, which include prizes accordingly.

THE CONCEPT CREW
(TREASURE HUNT)

Treasure hunts come in a large variety of types with different sets of rules. Some treasure hunts are a race to find the final clue and solve it. For others, each clue is worth points and sometimes offers the choice of completing one of two tasks. Basically there are three rounds in this event. During each round the winning contestants will be taken forward to the next level. Two rounds consist of finding and interpreting the clues which would be given to participants by the event coordinators. Difficulty level will increases with every round. We will provide appropriate number of clues if the participants fail to achieve the given task.

Rules
  • Participants have to take part as individual and as a team for further round.
  • Use of mobile phone is strictly prohibited.
  • An entire team must stay together or the team can split up to find different clues to help them on the treasure hunt.The team members should remain throughout the game in ABES Campus.
  • Teams have to keep the clues with them until the game finish for first round.
  • Total 30 minutes will be allotted to each team for second round.
  • Max 50 minutes will be allotted to each team for the third round.
  • After collecting the parts as per clues, the event coordinators will assess it instantly and give it back to the respective team.
  • Teams may pick up only one copy of the clue at each location. The team cannot hide or touch any of the copies intended for teams that arrive after they pick their own clue up. Doing this is cheating and results in disqualification.
  • Any damage done by participant to college property will result into instant disqualification from the event and a penalty may be levied too i.e. Participants will have to pay for the damage they incur.
  • Participants may take help from the helpers provided but then some points will be deducted from the final score of the team.
  • Team Projections is not to be held responsible for any physical injury that occurs to any of the participants.
  • Any internal conflicts between teams will result into instant dismissal of those teams.
  • Participants who exceed the given time limit will be disqualified. No excuses will be entertained.
  • Final decision will be given by judges only, No team or participant has the right to argue with judges or coordinators.
  • According to increase in levels/rounds the game area will be increased.
Phase 1

Know Your College

  • In this round, a quiz based on genero theme and know your college .
  • A Limited time of 20 minutes will be given to the each participants to submit their quiz.
  • Top 28 participants will be selected for further rounds.
Phase 2

The Coordination Round

  • In this round, clues, picture clues will be given. Area will be not so large in this round. Time is limited to 20 to 30 minutes to the reach the destination, as depicted in the clues and solve the puzzle.
  • Points will be counted on the basis of criteria of passing the clue stages AND this round also include some physical strength to go in the final round.
  • A maximum of 4 teams will qualify this round.
Phase 3

Finals

  • In this round, all the clues will be interrelated like a spider’s web, so in order to move to next clue you need to solve the first one. Apart from that some surprise elements will be revealed at the time of commencement of this round.
  • Teams which fail to solve the first clue will be disqualified at that point of time.
  • At last, the participants have to collect clues from all the rounds. Area includes full campus (as instructed by coordinator at the time of event). Time is limited to 60 minutes.
  • The Team which completes this round in the least time will be selected as winners.
