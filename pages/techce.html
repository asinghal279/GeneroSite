ROLLER COASTER

Roller coasters are thrill rides that operates like a railroad track. The history of this ride reflects a constant search for greater and more death-defying thrills. They are made from steel or wood and comprise a series of hills and drops, sudden turns, track loops and corkscrew-like elements. Roller coasters don’t have engines, they must be pulled by the gravity to the top of the first big hill. As the roller coaster rises higher, its potential energy increases and reaches maximum at crest of hill. The potential energy accumulated by the rise in height is transferred to kinetic energy as the cars race down the first downward slope. Teams shall design and build a “roller coaster” meeting the requirements of below rules. The “roller coaster” shall mean the entire structure, including the roller coaster track and the base, but not the actual vehicle. The “COASTER” shall mean the vehicle that travels on the Roller Coaster track.

Problem Statement

Teams need to make model of a roller coaster track using the basic materials like popsicle sticks, paper, fevicol. Commercially available roller coaster kits are not allowed to use.

MODEL RULES
  • Size restrictions - the height should not be more than 40cm.
  • The starting and stopping points must be clearly marked in the model.
  • The energy source for the ride can be gravitational pull only. Use of external energy sources like magnets, springs and electricity are not allowed.
  • Model should be consisting of at least one loop (horizontal or vertical).
JUDGING CRITERIA
  • Model should be in allowable size.
  • Object should be pass properly from starting point to end point.
Rules
  • Event is open to all. Maximum team size is 3.
  • Participants can form teams from different branches/college/ university/ institute having at least one civil engineering student.
  • No two teams must have any common member.
  • Teams are not allowed to touch their model once the ride begins.
  • The time limit for the completion is 180 minutes.
  • The decision of the judges shall be final and abiding.
  • Any coaster that violates the rule above or the spirit of the competition will be disqualified.

POPSIO-GANG-PLANK

A truss is a structure that consists of members organised into connected triangles so that the overall assembly behaves as a single object. Trusses are most commonly used in bridges, roofs and towers. A truss is made up of a web of triangles joined together to enable the even distribution of weight and the handling of changing tension and compression without bending or shearing. The triangle is geometrically stable when compared to a four (or more) -sided shape which requires that the corner joints are fixed to prevent shearing.

Rules
  • The load bearing capacity of the bridge will be checked (i.e. till the bridge breaks).
  • Maximum team size is 3.
  • The teams will be provided with Popsicle sticks and Fevicol, they have to prepare a Truss bridge model with them.
  • The strength (Load bearing capacity) will determined by applying load on the centre of the bridge with the help of hanger on second day.
  • Load will be applied on the deck of the bridge.
  • The dimension should not exceed (40 x 10 x10) cm (length*breath*height).Maximum allowable weight is 150gm.
  • In case of equal load bearing capacity, design will be considered for judgement.
